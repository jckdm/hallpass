My dreams exist in liminal spaces. I might find myself in a stairwell, under a bridge, behind a fence, on a balcony, through a peephole, in a taxi cab, a closet, or a tunnel. My dreams are anchored by a rotation of these flimsy backdrops. Never a destination, always a space to be passed through, or a threshold to be crossed over. I think of these spaces as hallways.

Many of the questions I ask about dreaming also apply to being awake. Stop and ask yourself how you are reading this sentence, how your hand moves a mouse, how your eyes dart across a screen. I am no more able to explain how I embody my waking ‘self’ than I am able to explain the actions of my dream ‘self’. Much like my dream locales are merely illusory channels through which I pass, are we not just series of electric pulses darting through hallways of the unknown?

I propose the metaphor of a ‘hall pass’ — a suggestion of authority, an unenforceable sign — to reframe my dreams as spaces of agency. Thus, my dream ‘self’ is not a prisoner, but a traveler with permission.

As we move through cities, we are bombarded by signs: signs underground telling us how to navigate, signs of love written by planes in the sky, signs in store windows that create desire. These signs are composed of words: words with obligations, words of warning or instruction, words often ignored.

This webpage draws visual inspiration from hanging LED displays in New York City subway stations with escalators. It emulates their circle-composed typeface and faded traffic-light color scheme. The words that flash across these subway signs are not to be interpreted, but followed. This webpage, however, reframes the instructional sign as a storytelling device, as a kind of hall pass for my dream ‘self’, and displays lines from a dream-poem that explores themes of dissociation.

I invite the viewer to linger for a moment in this space and to tug at the walls of their internet browser, to zoom in and out, to try and peek behind the curtain into a space that has no rules, that might be an allegory for life, or might mean nothing at all.

traveling with a suitcase filled with the things you left in your parents’ basement
unpacking yourself on the train
Passengers pass around pieces of your past
You miss your stop.
the trains arrive and the announcements grow longer each time
lay back and watch the graffiti twinkle on the tunnel walls
soldiers multiply in the windows’ reflections and they smile at you
You look at me and tell me you don’t know who i am but it is a pleasure to meet you, stranger.
I take you home. I ride the rails to pass the time.
I make friends with the other kids and no one asks where we’re going.
I get off at the next stop because my clothes start to unravel
You sleep all afternoon to remember who i am.
